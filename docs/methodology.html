Global shock identification

In the previous section I pointed out the tight relationship between "news-augmented" commodities price shocks and global shocks, and that we need to test if both generates a similar patron and/or if are the same. To achieve this objective, we should find a identification strategy that allows to clearly isolated global shocks. In that line, I first define what is understood as a global-shock for the scope of this paper

Definition 1: Let \(y^f\) being the observables that compound the foreign economy. A global shock is an underlying innovation related to persistent conditions that induces an instantaneous co-movement in every variable part of the external block and is based on primitive drivers.

The model

Let \(y_t^f\) and \(y_t\) be vectors of foreign and domestic variables, respectively. The reduce form for the two blocks VAR is $$ \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_t \\ y_t \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} B_{11,1} & 0 \\ B_{21,1} & B_{22,1} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_{t-1} \\ y_{t-1} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} B_{11,2} & 0 \\ B_{21,2} & B_{22,2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_{t-2} \\ y_{t-2} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \epsilon^f_{t} \\ \epsilon_{t} \end{bmatrix} $$
