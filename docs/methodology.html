Global shock identification

It is plausible to think that global variables respond to an underlying and deeper variable (or state of global economy)? or are the commodities movements really structural shocks? Regards those, literature has not a unique response. Many models and researchers treat commodity prices as exogenous variables for small open economies. The last could be consider as a weak assumption. On the other hand, since medium run identification was proposed and developed by Uhlig(2003) it has been used to identify anticipated shocks over a specific variable (fiscal policy, productivity, etc). In this sense, ZPV application surges like a natural, and very intuitively response to SGU results. The anticipated fraction of the augmented-news shock over terms of trade (or commodities prices) could reconcile the conclusion of SGU with the broadly idea about the importance of commodities for small open economies. However, what is a news shock? what is exactly a commodity news shock? The former was responded by XXX, \textit{``a news shock should be understand as any shock that changes the current information set of the implicit households in our models"}. On this way, we could consider a commodity news shock as any change in our beliefs that causes differences between that expected value based on a forward looking behavior and the effective expected value. Nevertheless, when we talk about commodities news shocks, the common understanding leads to think that they are new shocks that have their source in commodity prices, or at least are related mainly with them

The model

Let \(y_t^f\) and \(y_t\) be vectors of foreign and domestic variables, respectively. The reduce form for the two blocks VAR is $$ \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_t \\ y_t \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} B_{11,1} & 0 \\ B_{21,1} & B_{22,1} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_{t-1} \\ y_{t-1} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} B_{11,2} & 0 \\ B_{21,2} & B_{22,2} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} y^{f}_{t-2} \\ y_{t-2} \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} \epsilon^f_{t} \\ \epsilon_{t} \end{bmatrix} $$

Identification of Global Shocks

$$ \begin{aligned} & \underset{q_j}{\text{max}} & & \sum_{i \, \in \, y^f}\frac{S^{i}(\underline{\tau},\overline{\tau}|\gamma)}{S^{i}(\underline{\tau},\overline{\tau})} \\ & \text{s.t.} & & \gamma' * \gamma = 1 \\ & & & \gamma(i) = 0 \ \forall \ i \not\in y^f \end{aligned} $$ which can be re-expresed as: $$ \begin{aligned} & \underset{\gamma}{\text{max}} & & \gamma' \xi \gamma \\ & \text{s.t.} & & \gamma' * \gamma = 1\\ & & & \gamma(i) = 0 \ \forall \ i \not\in y^f \end{aligned} $$
