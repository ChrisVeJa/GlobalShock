Global Shocks

Heterogeneous Effects in Small Open Economies

In the literature it is well known that terms of trade movements have a sizable impact in domestic aggregates macroeconomics variables for Small Open Economies. Indeed, many economists build models that take them as a sufficient statistic for the world economy, but is it correct? In this research, using data for 10 countries and a novel identification strategy for global shocks I show that to adopt this posture is not correct and should be avoid at least for emerging SOEs. Moreover, I identify a second shock which is only related commodities prices movementes that I call as non-fundamental shocks. The results suggest that although global shocks are a good explanation source for terms of trade fluctuations, it is not the only one. On the other hand, in the short run the predictability power of these shocks differs between country groups being developed economies more fluctuations better explained. At summarize global shocks drive around one quarter of the predictability for domestic macro variables (as average in the analysed horizon) while non-fundamental innovations contribution is 10 percent smaller but with a share of 60 percent - on the short run - on terms of trade volatility. In particular, these shocks have different transmission mechanisms being clearer in effective exchange rates and real interest rates.
